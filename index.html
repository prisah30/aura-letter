To the woman who was told
it was all in her head.

I thought knowing the science would protect me. That if something was wrong, I'd be taken seriously. I sat in that room. The one where you walk in with questions and walk out with a prescription for yoga and a vague sense that you imagined it all.

I had the research in front of me. I knew what was happening. And I was still dismissed.

That moment broke something open in me. Because if it happened to me, someone who knew the language, what was happening to every woman who didn't?

I spoke to hundreds of you. The same story, over and over. High-functioning on the outside. Lost on the inside. Not because something was wrong with you. But because no one had ever helped you see the pattern.

You show up every single day. For your team, your partner, your children, your parents. You hold the schedule, carry the emotions, keep the wheels turning — and do it so seamlessly that no one sees the cost. Somewhere between being everything to everyone, you stopped having anything left for yourself. And when you finally fall quiet, you don't even know what you need anymore. That's not a flaw. That's what happens when you've spent your whole life using tools that were never built for you.

It's not inconsistency. It's rhythm. It's biology. It's you, and it's been there all along.

Aura is what I wish had existed. A companion that listens without judgment, remembers what you share, and quietly, over time, shows you who you actually are — so you can finally show up for yourself the way you've always shown up for everyone else.

We're opening beta to a small founding circle first. Women I trust. Women who'll tell us the truth. I'd be honoured if you were one of them.

With love, and a long overdue apology
on behalf of every system that failed you,
Priyanka
Co-founder, Aura · Scientist · One of you
